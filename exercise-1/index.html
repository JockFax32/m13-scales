Welcome to Scales Exercise 1

This demo illustrates how D3 leverages quantitative scales to translate from a continuous data-domain to a continuous output range. It is also an opportunity to practice rendering axis components to indicate the meaning of the scale, and walk through the standard data-join process for D3. In your js/main.js file, do the following:

  • Use d3.min and d3.max to determine the domain of your scale
  • Define a linear scale with your data domain and an output range of 50, 500
  • Select the SVG with id my-svg and assign it a width of 600 and a height of 100
  • Define an axis for your scale with the ticks oriented on the bottom
  • Append a g element with class axis to your svg. Shift it down 50 pixels by setting the transform property, and then use the .call method to render your axis in the g element.
  • Append a circle for each element in your data by using the standard D3 data binding process. Circles should be positioned horizontally based on their data (shoeSize) value.
